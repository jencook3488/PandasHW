
# __Analysis of PyCitySchools:__



1. It appears as though the smaller the size of the school, the greater the passing rate for students in both reading scores and math. The medium and small sized schools have a higher passing rate then the large schools by about 20%

2. School budget and student budgets seemed not to have an effect on the passing rate of the students. Students in the highest and lowest range of spending had very similar passing rates and schools with the higher budgets had lower passing rates.

3. Charter schools have a higher score in both categories and have a significantly higher overall passing rate at about 20% greater than District schools. This could also correlate to the size of the school since Charter School tend to be smaller in size.


```python
import pandas as pd
import numpy as np

schools = pd.read_csv('/Users/jennifercook/Downloads/UDEN201805DATA1-master 5/Week4/PandasHW/PyCitySchools/raw_data/schools_complete.csv')
students = pd.read_csv('/Users/jennifercook/Downloads/UDEN201805DATA1-master 5/Week4/PandasHW/PyCitySchools/raw_data/students_complete.csv')

school = schools.rename(columns={'name' : 'School','type' : 'Type',
                                 'size' : 'Size','budget' : 'Budget'})
student = students.rename(columns={'name' : 'Student','gender' : 'Gender',
                                   'grade' : 'Grade','school' : 'School',
                                   'reading_score' : 'Reading_Score',
                                   'math_score' : 'Math_Score'})

df = pd.merge(school, student, on=('School'))
```

```python
total_schools = df["School"].nunique()
```
